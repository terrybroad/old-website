(un)stable equilibrium

(un)stable equilibrium is an ongoing series of works that are borne out of an artistic practice developed around the training of generative neural networks without any training data. Much like traditional generative art, the architecture of the generative models and the toolkits of modern machine learning training are treated as a dynamical system that are explored intuitively for their latent aesthetic possibilities.



In the first series of works, the arrangement of networks is similar the Generative Adversarial Networks architecture. But for these works we have two generators that try to have their output passed of as the other networkâ€™s, while both competing to have more diversity in the colours they produce compared to the other network. Resulting in abstract, sometimes orthogonal compositions from the two generators. After training is completed, the resulting images from the two generators is presented side-by-side, as a video piece showing a synchronised interpolation between their respective latent spaces.



The piece (un)stable equilibrium 1:1 won the Grand prize at the ICCV 2019 computer vision art gallery. I am also going to be showing the work and presenting a paper at the NeurIPS 2019 Workshop on Machine Learning for Creativity and Design.
